\section{Including Figures}

Figures are included using the \texttt{graphicx} package. The figure environment
creates a "float" that LaTeX positions automatically.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.6\textwidth]{example_plot.png}
    \caption{An example plot generated by Python.}
    \label{fig:example}
\end{figure}

We can reference Figure~\ref{fig:example} anywhere in the document.

The placement specifier \texttt{[htbp]} suggests:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \texttt{h} - here (where it appears in source)
    \item \texttt{t} - top of page
    \item \texttt{b} - bottom of page
    \item \texttt{p} - separate page for floats
\end{itemize}

\section{Creating Tables}

Tables use the \texttt{tabular} environment for content and \texttt{table}
for floating with captions.

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{l|r|r}
        Asset & Return (\%) & Volatility (\%) \\
        \hline
        Stocks & 10.5 & 18.2 \\
        Bonds & 4.2 & 5.1 \\
        Cash & 2.0 & 0.5 \\
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Historical asset class characteristics.}
    \label{tab:assets}
\end{table}

Table~\ref{tab:assets} shows historical data.

\section{Column Alignment}

The tabular column specification controls alignment:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \texttt{l} - left aligned
    \item \texttt{c} - centered
    \item \texttt{r} - right aligned
    \item \texttt{|} - vertical line
\end{itemize}

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{lcc}
        \hline
        Name & Symbol & Price \\
        \hline
        Apple & AAPL & 150.00 \\
        Google & GOOGL & 2800.00 \\
        Microsoft & MSFT & 300.00 \\
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Stock prices (no vertical lines, cleaner look).}
    \label{tab:stocks}
\end{table}
